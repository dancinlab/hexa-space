## §1 Active programs (operating today)

### 1.1 Falcon 9 — workhorse
- **Status:** ramping toward 140–145 launches in CY2026 (Shotwell).
- **YTD (2026-05-03):** 53 F9 + 1 FH = 54 Falcon family launches.
- **Reuse milestone:** booster **B1067** flew flights 33 (Feb 21–22) and
  34 (Mar 30) — single-vehicle record. SpaceX certifying for 40 flights.
- **Pad shift:** Pad **39A** is being vacated for Dragon use → Starship
  takes over as Starship infrastructure builds out.

### 1.2 Falcon Heavy
- Flying NSSL Phase 3 Lane 2 missions through FY26.
- Recent: **ViaSat-3 F3** scrubbed Apr 27 (weather) → Apr 29 attempt.
- Award queue (Lane 2): **4 FH** missions added Oct 2025 batch (USSF-206
  WGS-12, USSF-155, USSF-149, USSF-63 + NROL-86; combined ~$714M).

### 1.3 Starlink (Gen-2 → V3)
- **Constellation:** ~10,374 birds in orbit, 10,358 active.
- **Mobile / Direct-to-Cell:** targeting **25M users by EOY 2026**;
  +52,000 net mobile adds/day projected.
- **V3 satellites:** first-half 2026 deployment via Starship; each
  V3 = >1 Tbps DL / >200 Gbps UL.
- **DTC upgrade path:** 150 Mbps target; phone-based testing late 2026 on
  EchoStar spectrum ($17B acquisition); 150 Mbps live ~late 2027.
- **FCC filing 2025-09:** request for **+15,000 LEO sats** for DTC.

### 1.4 Crew Dragon — NASA crew rotations
- **Crew-12** (Freedom): launched 2026-02-13 05:15 EST from CCSFS SLC-40;
  docked ISS 2026-02-14 ~15:15 EST. Crew: Meir, Hathaway, Adenot (ESA),
  Fedyaev (Roscosmos). Stay through Oct 2026.
- **Crew-13:** NET mid-September 2026 (advanced from November).
  Commander: **Jessica Watkins** — first NASA astronaut to fly Crew Dragon twice.

### 1.5 Cargo Dragon — CRS
- **CRS-32:** launched 2025-04-21 from 39A; 3,021 kg cargo.
- **CRS-33:** launched 2025-08-24 from SLC-40; included **boost-kit**
  trunk module (hydrazine + NTO + Draco thrusters, station re-boost demo);
  splashed down 2026-02-27 02:44 ET.

### 1.6 Rideshare cadence (Transporter / Bandwagon)
- **Transporter-16:** launched 2026-03-30 04:02 PT from VSFB SLC-4E
  (119 payloads).
- **Transporter-17:** NET 2026-06-30.
- Steady-state plan: ~3 Transporter + 2 Bandwagon dedicated rideshares/yr.

## §2 Upcoming / in-development

### 2.1 Starship V3 — Flight 12 (IFT-12)
- **Window opens:** 2026-05-12 22:30 UTC (CADENA advisory).
- **Hardware:** Ship 39 (V3) + Booster 19 (V3) + Raptor 3 engines.
- **Static fires done:** Ship 39 full-duration (April 14, 2026);
  Booster 19 partial 10-engine (mid-March), full 33-engine SF cleared
  path to flight (Apr 15–16, 2026).
- **Capability:** >100 t to LEO reusable (3× V2).
- **Pad:** Starbase **Pad 2** (chopsticks + 20 hold-down arms + chilldown
  vent, near-operational).

### 2.2 Starship orbital propellant transfer demo
- **Target:** 2026 (originally Mar 2026; slipping with V3 timeline).
- **Profile:** 2× Starship launches; tanker docks with depot ship in LEO
  and transfers cryogenic propellant.
- **Why critical:** **Starship HLS needs ~10 tanker launches** to refuel
  for lunar transit. No demo, no Artemis surface mission.

### 2.3 Artemis HLS (Starship HLS for NASA)
- **Status:** Artemis III rebaselined Feb 2026 → no longer surface
  landing; LEO rendezvous + docking demo NET late 2027 with both
  SpaceX HLS and Blue Moon.
- **Surface landing:** deferred to Artemis IV (early 2028) — lander
  selection contingent on rendezvous demo + design certification review
  (DCR was supposed to happen summer 2025 — *had not occurred as of Mar 2026*).

### 2.4 Mars uncrewed Starship window
- **Plan (Musk, May 2025):** 5× uncrewed Starship V3 → Mars during
  Nov–Dec 2026 window; payload includes **Tesla Optimus** humanoid robots.
- **Confidence:** Musk publicly stated ~50% chance of being ready.
- **2026-02-09 update:** Mars goal pushed back **5–7 years** to focus on
  Moon.
- **Subsequent waves:** ~20 missions in 2028/29 window; first crewed
  Mars NET 2029; self-sustaining colony goal 2050.

### 2.5 Polaris Program
- **Polaris II (in development):** Falcon 9 + Crew Dragon. Schedule
  uncertain — Isaacman confirmed as **NASA Administrator** (Dec 18, 2025).
- **Polaris III (future):** **first crewed Starship flight** — TBD.

### 2.6 Vast Haven-1 (commercial space station)
- **Launch:** NET **May 2026**, Falcon 9 to LEO.
- **Crew transport:** Crew Dragon.
- **Specs:** 4.4 m dia · 45 m³ habitable volume · Starlink laser-link
  Gigabit downlink (first commercial station with Starlink).
- **Ops plan:** 4 crewed missions (4 people × ~10 days) over 3-yr life.
- **Successor:** **Haven-2** depends on NASA CLD program selection.

### 2.7 Axiom private astronaut missions
- **Ax-5:** awarded Jan 30 2026; NET January 2027 (Crew Dragon, ~2 weeks).
- **Ax-6:** mission order being finalized (NASA + Vast also got a slot).

### 2.8 Dragonfly (NASA Titan rotorcraft)
- **Launch vehicle:** Falcon Heavy.
- **Window:** 2028-07-05 → 2028-07-25.
- **Contract:** $256.6M.

### 2.9 Lunar Gateway launch
- **Vehicle:** Falcon Heavy ($331.8M, awarded 2021).
- **Status:** NASA **sun-setting Gateway** to refocus on direct-to-surface
  exploration — element launch may be cancelled or descoped.

### 2.10 NSSL Phase 3 — military backlog
- **Lane 2 share:** SpaceX = Requirement 1 (~60% / ~28 missions through
  FY29); ULA ~19; Blue Origin ≤7.
- **FY26 awards:** 5× SpaceX (USSF + NRO) vs 2× ULA.
- **Lane 1 awards:** $739M task orders to SpaceX.
- **GPS:** final **GPS III** flew on Falcon 9 (Apr 21, 2026); next-gen
  **GPS IIIF-4** assigned to ULA Vulcan (USSF-88).

### 2.11 Launch infrastructure build-out
- **Starbase Pad 2 (TX):** near operational (chilldown vents, 20 HDAs,
  upgraded chopsticks).
- **LC-39A (FL):** SQD arm rolled out; tanks/subcoolers/cryo plumbing in
  progress; first Starship launch from 39A target = **2H 2026**.
- **SLC-37 (FL):** 2 towers planned.
- **Aggregate cap:** 5 launch pads → Starship cadence approval up to
  **146 launches/yr** across 39A + SLC-37 + Starbase.
- **FAA:** Final EA + FONSI/ROD issued for additional Boca Chica
  trajectories + RTLS profiles; license modification still pending.
